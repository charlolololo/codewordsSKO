Coding has always been an area that I did my best to avoid, as I have always been weak in areas

What is creative coding?

Initially, I had no idea what to expect as my only impression of creative coding came from the
Codewords 2020 website. I tried searching for examples on my own but did not even know what
kind of key words to use. The examples of creative coding given by Andy really helped to set a better
perspective of what exactly we could create with coding. While the whole concept still seems rather
abstract to me, I now have more ideas on what one can create through coding.

One of the creatives introduced that left an impression on me was Winnie Soon from Hong Kong.
As a Singaporean-Chinese, designs with Chinese characters often evoke a sense of nostalgia in me
as it reminds me of home. Upon a closer look, I realised that I had chanced upon her work last year in ACCA!

Concrete Poetry

After exploring the basics of creative coding we were introduced to the idea of concrete poetry. With Mark Amerika's “OK OK” texts, we played around with layout for concrete poetry.

Afterwards, we were challenged to use a short phrase that we could use to construct our own concrete poetry. I have a friend who always likes to jokingly say, “Don't be sorry, be better,” whenever one of us in the friend group messes up. Hence, I chose to use the first half of the phrase for my concrete poetry experimentation.

Introduction to HTML

Before going straight into coding with HTML, we had to understand what exactly was WWW and the Anatomy of a Webpage.
In my previous jobs and internships when I had to work with areas such as SEO optimisation, I was heavily confused,
but learning what goes on behind every webpage now helps everything make sense.

After understanding the basics, we finally began to code in Visual Studio Code. Even the set-up of the application was
overwhelming, as I am not used to the large amount of texts compared to just looking at an Adobe Suite.

While most of the information can already be found on the W3Schools website, here are some notes from what we learnt

The first thing we coded on Visual Studio Code was setting up a basic page; our very own “Hello World” page.We explored basic styling as well as the concept between divs, headers and body text. This section was rather overwhelming, as I was basically learning a whole new language. While this page looks old-fashioned, it contains a lot of important basic tags such as <div> and the various styling that I am still relatively new to. After setting up a basic page, we explored how to change elements on the page such as repeating the background, making it responsive as well as distorting text.
